About this lab

The Virtual Biophysical Lab is a small, self-contained space for experimenting with biophysical models used in vegetation remote sensing. It is intentionally minimal: no heavy framework, no complex web app – just simple scripts, examples and notes.

The focus is on concepts such as radiative transfer, plant traits and scaling from leaf to canopy and scene level. The lab can support both research prototyping and teaching activities (e.g. short exercises, demos or workshops).

Typical components you might host in this repo:

  • Simple RTM runs (e.g. PROSAIL-style or SCOPE-style examples).
  • Synthetic datasets of reflectance, SIF or temperature.
  • Small R or Python notebooks exploring model parameters.
  • Figures and plots used in teaching slides or quick analyses.

Modules and topics

The exact structure of the lab is flexible. A possible organisation of folders/modules is:

  • rtm/ – basic radiative transfer model examples (leaf and canopy).
  • traits/ – simple simulations linking biochemical/structural traits to spectra.
  • time-series/ – toy models of diurnal or seasonal dynamics.
  • notebooks/ – R / Python notebooks for interactive exploration.
  • figures/ – plots and diagrams generated by the lab.
  • docs/ – notes for students or short explanations of exercises.

You can adapt this layout to fit your own workflow and keep only what is useful.

Example use cases

Teaching and exercises

Use the lab as a companion to short tutorials on:

  • How changing LAI or chlorophyll affects canopy reflectance.
  • Simple simulations of drought or stress effects on spectra.
  • Visualising synthetic vs. real satellite bands.

Research prototyping

Keep lightweight notebooks and scripts to quickly test ideas before integrating them into larger processing pipelines, such as:

  • Trying a new index or spectral feature.
  • Exploring parameter sensitivity in an RTM.
  • Generating small lookup tables for trait retrieval tests.

Getting started

A minimal workflow for using this repository could be:

  1. Clone the repository or download it as a zip file.
  2. Open the notebooks/ or rtm/ folders in your IDE of choice.
  3. Run a simple script and modify a few parameters (e.g. LAI, Cab, soil brightness).
  4. Plot the results and compare them across scenarios.
